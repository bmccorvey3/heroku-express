Instructions

To explore and change the Digital Divide:

1. Hover over a country to view its name, National Technological Rank (in 2011) and IDI (in 2011).

2. Double click on the map to zoom in.

3. Press escape to zoom out.

4. Click on a country to select it OR search for a country by name in the search bar to select it. The name of the selected country will appear above the map. Note: The country will not remain outlined if the mouse is no longer hovering over it, but the program has registered your selection.

5. To “send” resources to the selected country, click one of the buttons above the map.

6. A pop-up window will notify you of the cost associated with the selected resource and ask you to specify how much of the resource to send. Choose amount above 10,000 for noticeable changes in IDI value.

7. Hover over the selected country again to view the updated IDI value and compare it to the country’s original IDI (in 2011).

8. If the IDI has increased enough, the color of the country will change to reflect where it now falls with regard to the Digital Divide.

9. Notice that the “Cost” has increased to reflect the total amount of money spent on sending resources.

10. To continue sending resources to the selected country, repeat steps 6-9.

11. To select a new country, simply click on the desired country or search for a country in the search bar and follow steps 4-9. Note: The “Cost” does not reset for each country but continues to increase until the entire map is reset.

12. To reset the map, return all IDI values to their original value and all countries to their original colors, click the reset button.

Exercise 1: Comparing the Effect of Populations

Select Venezuela (located in the northern part of South America), which has an IDI value of 3.92. “Send” 500,000 computers to Venezuela. Now select China (located in Asia), which as an IDI value of 3.88 and “send” 500,000 computers. Venezuela and China started with similar IDI values (in 2011), but China’s population is almost 5 times larger than Venezuela’s population. Compare updated IDI values for the two countries. Which country’s IDI increased more? Why? What does this mean about bridging the Digital Divide?

Exercise 2: Comparing the Effect of Resources

1. Select Algeria (located in North Africa) and send enough computers to make every household have a computer. Look at the increase in IDI. What was the cost?

2. Reset the map. Now increase Internet access until everyone has Internet access. Look at the increase in IDI. What was the cost?

3. Reset the map. Now send enough mobile phones so more than enough people have mobile phones. Look at the increase in IDI. What was the cost?

4. Reset the map. Now provide Algeria with enough fixed broadband. Look at the increase in IDI. What was the cost?

5. Reset the map. Now provide Algeria with education until it's fully literate. Look at the increase in IDI. What was the cost?

6. Compare the increase in IDI to the costs for each method. What do the results mean?
