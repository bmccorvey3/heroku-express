Welcome to the Digital Divide Explorer!

This interactive program is designed to increase awareness and understanding of the digital divide. It allows the user to visually explore the digital divide through:

  • changing the important variables influencing the digital divide, such as access to computers, internet, mobile-phones, fixed broadband subscriptions, and education
  • viewing the resulting effect on the country's technological development
  • comparing countries around the world.

The digital divide refers to the gap in levels of technological advancement between developed and developing countries, and in some cases, between populations within a single country.

Technological advancement is often measured by access to, use of, and knowledge of Information and Communication Technologies (ICTs), such as computers, the Internet, and telephones. The spread of ICTs, especially in developing countries, can be a valuable tool to help bridge the digital divide.

In order to track technological development, the United Nations International Telecommunications Union collects and analyzes data on ICT access and use in over 150 countries and publishes the results in an annual report. Countries are ranked based on an index calculated from statistics gathered for 11 “indicators.” This program uses data published by the United Nations International Telecommunications Union in their 2012 report.
